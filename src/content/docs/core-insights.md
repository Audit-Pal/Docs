# Core Insights

## Static Auditing

The best documentation for smart contract exploiters. The current process works like this: once a smart contract is ready on testnet, the protocol reaches out to an auditing firm. The firm reviews the code, flags concerns at three severity levels, medium, high, and critical, and passes a report back to the development team. The team fixes what is flagged, and the contract launches. That is where security ends.

New vulnerabilities emerge overnight. Attack patterns evolve constantly. Once a contract is live on mainnet, it is on its own. Every major exploit in history happened long after the contracts went into operation. For example, the last audit of Yearn Finance (yUSD) token was in 2020, yet it was hacked in 2023. A constructor misconfiguration lay dormant for roughly 1,000 days before an attacker exploited it, stealing approximately $11.6 million. In 2025 alone, $2.1 billion was lost to attacks on deployed contracts.

## Speed

The auditing workflow is almost entirely manual. Auditors work through several AI tools and frameworks, but the process itself has not fundamentally changed. For basic projects, an audit takes one to two days. For complex protocols, it takes one to two weeks. On top of that, top firms carry waitlists of two to four weeks on average. Protocols are waiting a month just to get started.

## Cost

Using AI is cheaper than a team of expert auditors. The more demand, the higher the price. Basic project audits run between $10,000 and $15,000. Advanced protocol audits run between $50,000 and $100,000. As on-chain velocity increases and more contracts need review, costs climb with demand rather than falling with efficiency.
